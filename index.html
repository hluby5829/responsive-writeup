Interface Description

For my public interface, I chose the ordering kiosk inside of Ten One Tea House. It's available for public use but not a necessity in every day life (although some may disagree).

Here are sketches of the main screens a user sees while ordering:

Screen 1. Lets the user know it's a touch pad and they can place an order here.

Screen 2. Continuous scroll to browse menu. Users can filter, search, and see item info here. They can also access their cart using the ever-present blue button.

Screen 3. Shows all the items in the user's cart along with a customizations and running total. Lets users edit their cart and continue browsing items.

Screen 4. Prompts the user to pay using the card reader. Interactive component switches from the touch pad to the card reader located below the screen.

Observations

Interview Questions

It seemed like most people going to Ten One were either picking up a Snackpass order or ordering from the kiosk, skipping the in-person interaction entirely.

For all interviewees who agreed to give feedback, I asked the following questions:

  • How was your experience navigating the order screen?
  • Were you able to find and order the drink you originally wanted to get?
  • Why did you choose to use the ordering kiosk instead of the counter?
Summary
  • Most people knew right away that they were supposed to touch to order. Some would also look at the physical menu at the counter before using the kiosk.
  • A lot of first time users could not find the card reader when it came time to pay.
  • Of all the people I interviewed, none mentioned any challenges interacting with the screen, even after I observed some of them looking for the card reader.
  • The most common answer for why they chose to use the kiosk was that it was more convenient.

Persona Empathy Maps

Browsing Betty

Browsing Betty is new to Ten One but not to boba. The problem she has is that she's never interacted with this particular ordering kiosk before. She represents a first time user who knows they like boba but don't go too often, which I found to be common during user interviews.

Impatient Ian

Impatient Ian knows exactly what they want when they go to Ten One. The problem they have is there is no "past order" or favorites filtering available on the kiosk even though it is already connected through Snackpass. They represent a user who frequents Ten One and the ordering kiosk.

Storyboards

Browsey Betty is coming back from a pretty tough exam. She is pretty drained but relieved it's over.

While walking back to her dorm she passes Ten One Tea and gets a fantastic idea to treat herself to some boba after all her hard work.

Betty walks in and chooses the ordering kiosk because she hasn't been here in awhile and wants to browse without talking to the bobarista.

She walks up to the kiosk and taps the screen to begin.

Scrolling through, Betty has a ton of drink options. She scrolls vertically through the menu and clicks some filters.

She's found a drink that sounds good and takes her time comparing the different topping options. Then she checks out.

The screen tells her to continue to the card reader but Betty can't seem to find it since this is her first time using the kiosk.

After tapping the screen a couple times, she finally notices the card reader just below the screen.

With her drink paid for, Betty makes her way to the counter to wait for her drink.

Success! The drink is just what she needed to get her mind off that exam. Betty is glad she took a some extra time to compare drink options, too. This might be her new favorite!
